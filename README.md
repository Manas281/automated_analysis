**README**

**Dataset Overview**

This dataset contains information about various countries across the world. It has 2363 rows and 11 columns, with each row representing a country in a specific year. The columns provide insights into different aspects of a country's well-being, such as its life expectancy, GDP per capita, social support, and more.

**Analysis Performed**

We analyzed the dataset to identify trends and patterns in the data. Our analysis focused on the following aspects:

* Distribution of missing values: We identified the columns with the most missing values and explored possible reasons for the missing data.
* Correlation between variables: We calculated the correlation between different variables to understand how they relate to each other.
* Trends over time: We analyzed the changes in the variables over time to identify any patterns or trends.

**Key Observations**

Based on our analysis, we observed the following key points:

* The column with the most missing values is 'Generosity', with 81 missing values. This suggests that there may be issues with data collection or reporting for this variable.
* The correlation between 'Log GDP per capita' and 'Life Ladder' is strong, indicating that GDP per capita is a significant predictor of a country's well-being.
* There is a positive trend in 'Healthy life expectancy at birth' over time, suggesting that countries are making progress in improving the health of their citizens.
* The correlation between 'Positive affect' and 'Negative affect' is negative, indicating that countries with higher positive affect tend to have lower negative affect.

**Conclusion**

Our analysis provides insights into the trends and patterns in the dataset. The strong correlation between GDP per capita and life ladder suggests that economic development is a key driver of well-being. The positive trend in healthy life expectancy at birth is a positive sign, but the high number of missing values in the 'Generosity' column raises concerns about data quality. Further analysis is needed to explore these findings in more detail and to identify areas for improvement.
